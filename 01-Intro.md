# Introduction to Security Engineering

This document summarizes the foundational concepts from the *Introduction to Security Engineering* module. It covers what Security Engineers do, their core responsibilities, and the continuous processes required to maintain an organization’s security posture.

---

## What Is a Security Engineer?

A Security Engineer is responsible for protecting an organization's digital assets and maintaining its overall security posture.

### Key Functions
- Design secure systems and architectures  
- Identify, minimize, and manage vulnerabilities  
- Perform audits, tests, and continuous monitoring  
- Build defenses that are resilient and difficult to bypass  

---

## Core Responsibilities

### Asset Management  
Maintain an accurate inventory of digital assets.  
You cannot protect what you don’t know exists.

### Security Policies  
Develop and maintain organization-wide policies based on proven security principles.

### Secure-by-Design  
Architect systems so they are inherently resistant to attacks, not patched as an afterthought.

### Security Assessment & Assurance  
Regularly test and validate systems to strengthen defenses as threats evolve.

---

## Continuous Improvement

### Security Awareness  
Train employees to recognize cyber threats like phishing and social engineering.

### Risk Management  
Evaluate risks, determine which must be accepted, and advise leadership on mitigation strategies.

### Change Management  
Assess new changes or deployments to determine how they affect the security posture.

### Vulnerability Management  
Track, prioritize, and remediate vulnerabilities — starting with the highest impact.

### Compliance & Audits  
Ensure the organization meets laws, regulations, and industry standards.

---

## Additional Responsibilities

### Incident Response Support  
Assist in investigations, root-cause analysis, and post-incident improvements.

### Tooling & Automation  
Develop or integrate tools and scripts that automate security tasks and increase efficiency.
